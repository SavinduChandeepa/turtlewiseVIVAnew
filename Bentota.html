Bentota Hatchery

The importance of Bentota Turtle Hatchery

Six of the seven species of sea turtles are now in risk of extinction. The majority of it results from the exploitation of natural resources by humans. Thankfully, this hatchery has accepted responsibility for their protection. Beginning with watching over the nesting mother turtles, they next transport their eggs to the safe environment of the hatchery and, on the day of birth, release the young turtles into the sea. If there are problems, they tend to them and only release them when they are well enough to enter the huge ocean.All five of the country's species can be found making their nests at Kosgoda, one of the relatively few hatcheries. They are the Hawksbill, Leatherback, Green, and Olive Ridley turtles.

The Experience

Hikkaduwa Hatchery offers a unique and engaging experience for visitors. Visitors can observe various stages of the sea turtle lifecycle, from nesting and egg incubation to the release of hatchlings into the ocean. Guided tours provide insight into the hatchery's conservation work, allowing visitors to witness the efforts being made to protect these magnificent creatures. There are also opportunities to participate in turtle releases, where visitors can actively contribute to the conservation of sea turtles.

Watching Turtles Eggs Hatch

seeing the hatching of turtle eggs and releasing the hatchling turtles into the water. This is a rare chance to observe the life cycle of these threatened species and to contribute to their survival in their native habitat. It is possible to observe the process of removing the eggs from the beach and transporting them to the hatchery, where they are buried in sand until they hatch. You may observe the delicate handling of the young turtles as well as their nighttime release into the water, when they stand a greater chance of escaping predators.

Education & Awareness

finding out about conservation activities and turtle habitats. You can learn more about the various types of turtles, their behaviors, dangers, and strategies for protecting them. Additionally, you can converse with and gain knowledge from the hatchery's staff and volunteers. You can look at videos, read posters, ask questions, and see the turtles in their aquariums. Additionally, you can discover the difficulties and achievements of turtle conservation in Sri Lanka and other countries.

Volunteer opportunities

volunteering for a range of initiatives, including egg collection, turtle care, and public outreach. By taking part in various duties and activities that promote turtle conservation, you may assist the hatchery's objective. Along the road, you can also learn new talents and meet new people. You can participate with the cleaning and feeding of the turtles in the hatchery, the medical care and rehabilitation of damaged turtles, or the project's awareness- and fund-raising efforts. You can also join the night patrols that search the beach for nesting turtles.

identifying different turte species

seeing various turtle species, including loggerhead, leatherback, green, and olive ridley. You may get a close-up look at these magnificent creatures and take in their variety and beauty. You can see how their shells vary in terms of size, shape, color, and pattern. Additionally, you may observe how they respond in various contexts, such as while eating, sleeping, swimming, or engaging with one another. Additionally, you can witness some unique and uncommon turtles, such as white or albino turtles.

How to Visit

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is situated around 70 kilometers from Colombo city in a seaside town on Sri Lanka's southwestern coast. It was established in 1981 and is one of 18 turtle hatcheries on the southern coast of the nation. It is simple to travel there by car, using either the Colombo-Galle A2 or the E01 Southern Express highway
