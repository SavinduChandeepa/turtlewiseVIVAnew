The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Hikkaduwa Hatchery is a renowned turtle conservation facility located in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka. This hatchery plays a vital role in the protection and preservation of endangered sea turtles, as well as raising awareness about their conservation needs. It is a popular destination for both locals and tourists seeking to learn about these magnificent creatures and contribute to their well-being.

The hatchery focuses on various aspects of sea turtle conservation. One of its primary objectives is the rescue and rehabilitation of injured or stranded turtles. The dedicated team at Hikkaduwa Hatchery works tirelessly to provide medical care, nursing the turtles back to health, and ensuring their safe return to the ocean. This aspect of their work is critical in mitigating the threats faced by sea turtles and giving them a fighting chance for survival.

In addition to rehabilitation, Hikkaduwa Hatchery is actively involved in the protection of sea turtle nests. The team carefully monitors nesting sites, safeguarding the eggs from potential threats such as predators and human interference. They employ strategies to enhance hatching success and ensure the safe journey of hatchlings to the sea. By protecting these nests, the hatchery significantly contributes to the growth and stability of sea turtle populations.

The Experience

>Hikkaduwa Hatchery offers a unique and engaging experience for visitors. Visitors can observe various stages of the sea turtle lifecycle, from nesting and egg incubation to the release of hatchlings into the ocean. Guided tours provide insight into the hatchery's conservation work, allowing visitors to witness the efforts being made to protect these magnificent creatures. There are also opportunities to participate in turtle releases, where visitors can actively contribute to the conservation of sea turtles.

Guided Tours

The guided tours at Hikkaduwa Hatchery offer a captivating and educational experience for visitors. Led by knowledgeable and passionate guides, these tours provide valuable insights into the world of sea turtles and their conservation. Visitors have the opportunity to witness the nesting process, observe turtle hatchlings, and learn about the challenges faced by these magnificent creatures. The guided tours cover various aspects of sea turtle conservation, including rescue and rehabilitation efforts, nest protection, and the importance of sustainable practices. Through engaging presentations, interactive exhibits, and up-close encounters with sea turtles, these tours leave visitors with a deep appreciation for these incredible marine creatures and inspire them to become advocates for their conservation.

Turtle Feeding and Rehabilitation

Turtle feeding and rehabilitation at Hikkaduwa Hatchery is a vital aspect of their conservation efforts. The hatchery provides specialized care and nourishment to injured or sick sea turtles, with the goal of restoring their health and preparing them for their return to the wild. Skilled staff members carefully monitor and administer proper diets tailored to each turtle's needs, ensuring they receive the nutrients necessary for their recovery. Through this rehabilitation process, the hatchery plays a crucial role in giving these turtles a second chance at life, ultimately contributing to the preservation and sustainability of sea turtle populations.

Turtle Hatchling Releases

One of the most anticipated and magical moments at Hikkaduwa Hatchery is the turtle hatching releases. As the hatchlings emerge from their nests, visitors are treated to an awe-inspiring sight as these tiny creatures make their way towards the ocean. Guided by the moonlight, the hatchlings scramble across the sand, propelled by an innate instinct to reach the water. It is a heartwarming experience to witness their determination and resilience as they embark on their perilous journey. The hatchery staff, along with excited visitors, cheer them on, ensuring their safe passage and sending them off with well wishes for a future filled with possibilities. These hatching releases at Hikkaduwa Hatchery serve as a powerful reminder of the importance of our collective efforts in safeguarding the survival of sea turtles and preserving their natural habitats.

Education & Awareness

Education and awareness play a crucial role at Hikkaduwa Hatchery in Sri Lanka. The hatchery is committed to enlightening visitors about the significance of sea turtle conservation and the challenges faced by these remarkable creatures. Through guided tours, interactive exhibits, and informative sessions, visitors gain valuable insights into the life cycle of sea turtles, their nesting habits, and the importance of protecting their habitats. The educational programs at Hikkaduwa Hatchery aim to inspire a sense of responsibility and empower individuals to become advocates for the conservation of sea turtles and the marine environment as a whole. By raising awareness and fostering a deeper understanding, Hikkaduwa Hatchery is making a significant impact in shaping a more sustainable future for these extraordinary species.

Volunteer opportunities

You will have the chance to work closely with the dedicated staff, gaining hands-on experience in sea turtle rehabilitation and protection. This rewarding experience allows you to make a tangible difference in the lives of these incredible creatures while immersing yourself in the beauty of Sri Lanka's coastal ecosystem. Whether you're a student, researcher, or simply an animal lover, volunteering at Hikkaduwa Hatchery offers a unique opportunity to learn, grow, and make a positive impact on the conservation of sea turtles.
